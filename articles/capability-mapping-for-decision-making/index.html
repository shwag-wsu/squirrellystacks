Capability Mapping for Decision-Making

Enterprise architecture • Planning • Capability maps

Capability maps are most valuable when they improve prioritization and architecture conversations. They are much less valuable when they turn into a large taxonomy exercise disconnected from funding and delivery choices.

Problem

Teams often jump straight into application or platform discussions before agreeing on which business capability is actually under pressure. That leads to investment choices that optimize systems before clarifying outcomes.

Practical approach

  1. Identify the business outcome that needs improvement.
  2. Map the supporting capabilities at a level leadership can still discuss.
  3. Highlight weak spots, maturity gaps, and dependencies.
  4. Use the map to guide roadmap choices, not to replace them.

What to watch for

  • Avoid over-detailing the map too early.
  • Keep ownership and business language visible.
  • Link capability issues to real delivery constraints and system boundaries.
