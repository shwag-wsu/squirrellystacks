Architecture Artifact Starter Kit

Enterprise architecture • Templates • Reusable assets

Most teams do not need more architecture documentation. They need a smaller set of artifacts that are easy to reuse, easy to explain, and useful in planning, review, and delivery conversations.

Starter kit mindset

A good starter kit does not try to solve every architecture scenario. It gives teams a practical baseline: a current-state / target-state outline, a lightweight review note, and a simple decision record that captures rationale without becoming ceremony-heavy.

Three artifacts worth standardizing

  1. Architecture overview: context, goals, scope, key constraints, and major dependencies.
  2. Decision record: the choice made, why it was made, and what could force a revisit.
  3. Review note: findings, follow-up actions, risks, and next steps.

What makes a template reusable

  • It is short enough to be adopted without training.
  • It reflects the language delivery teams already use.
  • It can link cleanly into standards, governance, and landscape views.

Template links across the repository

  • Reference library for reusable asset organization
  • Governance log for review and decision capture
  • Landscape section for current-state and target-state framing
  • Standards section for policy alignment and exceptions
