Architecture Landscape

Landscape views help anchor strategy in reality. They show the current operating environment, where the major pain points sit, and what a useful target state should change first.

Current-state focus

Start by describing the most important business capabilities, the systems that support them, key integration paths, and the parts of the estate creating risk or drag. The goal is not to model everything. The goal is to make the important parts visible enough to discuss tradeoffs.

Target-state focus

Target-state views should show better boundaries, better data flow, and less accidental complexity. They should be specific enough to influence delivery but lightweight enough to update as assumptions change.

Useful landscape slices

Business

Capabilities, ownership, and pain points that matter for prioritization.

Application

System boundaries, integration responsibilities, and modernization candidates.

Data

Critical entities, system of record concerns, and downstream analytics needs.
