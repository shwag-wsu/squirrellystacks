Governance Log

Architecture governance should leave behind a useful trail: what was reviewed, what was decided, what was deferred, and what assumptions need to be revisited. This section is the working memory for that trail.

What to capture

  • Architecture decisions and tradeoffs
  • Review outcomes and required follow-up actions
  • Exceptions to standards and their expiry points
  • Open questions that could change the chosen direction

Why this is valuable

A governance log reduces repeated debate, helps new team members understand past choices, and keeps architecture from feeling like a black box. It also makes future reassessment easier because the original context is still visible.

Recommended log structure

  1. Date and context of the review
  2. Decision or outcome
  3. Key rationale and constraints
  4. Risks, exceptions, and follow-up owners
